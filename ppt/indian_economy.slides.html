Introduction to GDP¶

What is Gross Domestic Product (GDP) ?¶

  • A measure of value of all goods and services produced in a country
  • Reflects economic activity
  • Calculating GDP: 3 different Ways (Production, Income, Expenditure)

Nominal vs. Real GDP¶

  • Nominal GDP: GDP at current prices
  • Real GDP: GDP at constant prices relative to a base year
  • Example: Consider increase in Petrol prices in 2020 and 2021
  • Real GDP = Nominal GDP - Inflation
  • When govt. says 5 trillion dollar GDP one should ask whether nominal or real

GDP in itself is not very representative of the economy¶

Per Capita GDP¶

  • Large population means large GDP
  • Use Per Capita GDP = ${\frac{\text{GDP}}{\text{Population}}}$

Per Capita GDP Purchasing Power Parity (PPP)¶

  • Purchasing power parity: Accounts for difference in prices in different countries
  • Example:
    1. Buying 1 Kg rice in Sweden (25 SEK) vs. India (50 INR)
    2. Renting Apartment in Sweden (7000 SEK) vs. India (14000 INR)
  • A measure of effective exchange rate for an individual

India's GDP¶

  • ~2.9 Trillion USD in 2019
  • Third largest GDP (PPP) (China ranks 1, US ranks 2)
  • India ranks 124 th in per capita GDP (PPP) (US: 7, China: 73, Germany: 15, Sweden: 16)
  • Purchasing power of individuals is very less in India and China compared to US, Sweden or Germany
  • Poverty due to underemployment (low paid and low skill jobs)

How can India can achieve high GDP growth rate ?¶

GDP growth rate $ = \frac{\Delta G}{G} = \frac{\Delta K}{G} * \frac {\Delta G}{\Delta K}$ = $ \text {investment-GDP ratio} * \eta $

2 factors:

  1. $\Delta K$ is increase in capital investment in a given year
  2. $ \eta $ is efficiency of capital use

How government policies influence $\Delta{K}$ ?¶

$${\Delta K}=\Delta{K_{private}} + \Delta{K_{public}} $$
  • Private investment: Spending your money for your company (more responsibility)
  • Public investment: Giving your money to govt. who gives it to someone else for spending (less reponsibility)
  • Limitations of public investment:
  1. High Public Investment Means Heavy Taxes on Poor and Middle Class
  2. Inefficient
  3. Corruption and Favoritism
  • Drawbacks of private investment:
  1. Labor Exploitation (శ్రమ దోపిడీ)
  2. Lack of employee welfare

How government policies influence ${\eta}$ ?¶

Exogeneous Economic Growth Model¶

  • Developed by Prof. Robert Solow (1981 Nobel Memorial Prize in Economics)
  • GDP growth rate is limited by Law of diminishing marginal returns
  • Only 35 % growth is due to ${\Delta K}$; 65% growth rate is due to $\eta$ (which is a function of innovation)
  • Innovation is essential to increase $\eta$
  • But considered as an external factor of the economic model

How government policies influence ${\eta}$ ?¶

Endogeneous Economic Growth Model¶

  • Prof. Michael Romer (2018 Nobel Memorial Prize in Economics, former IMF president)
  • Integrates innovation into exogeneous economic model

$$\eta = \eta_{business} + \eta_{tech}$$¶

  1. $\eta_{business}$: Jio, Patanjali, Vivo/Oppo (short-term)
  2. $\eta_{tech}$: Aeroplane, Computer, Internet, Social Networking, ML, AI, Genomics, Quantum Computers (long-term)
  • Sustainable economic growth possible by increasing incentive for sceintific innovation by spending more on education and R&D
  • India's spending on R & D is only 0.6-0.8 % of GDP, China spends 2.1%, US spends 2.8%, Israel spends 4.3%

Conclusions¶

  1. Increase private investment ($\Delta{K_{private}}$) by tax incentives to corporates that follow optimal wage policies/employee welfare
  2. Minimize government investment ($\Delta{K_{public}}$): income tax can be abolished and taxes on fuel can be decreased (Dr. Swamy's advice)
  3. Increase Business Innovation ($\eta_{business}$) by privatisation
  4. Foster Scientific Innovation ($\eta_{tech}$) by huge government spending (6% of GDP) on Education, Health and R&D.

Final Thoughts¶

  1. "Government must be an umpire not a player of the game" - Milton Friedman
  2. "Socialism means a large government, and a large government means heavy taxation that kills incentive for hard work." -Subramanian Swamy
  3. "The only way to achieve equity in India is to create adequate employment and ensure certain minimum amenities (like housing and transportation) for those employed. The taxation system should be used as an instrument to give incentives to capitalists for providing all these facilities" -Subramanian Swamy
